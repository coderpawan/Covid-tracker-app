Important Questions Asked

Private hospitals to charge Rs. 250 for Covid Vaccines

1) Can I get a vaccination without an appointment?

The government says that citizens aged 18+ should mandatorily register themselves and schedule appointments online before vaccination.
However currently On-spot registration or walk-in facility is currently not allowed at private hospitas for the 18+ years age group.

2) How many people can be registered in the Co-WIN portal through one mobile number?

Up to four people can be registered for vaccination using the same mobile number.

3) Can I register for vaccination without an Aadhaar card?

Yes, you can register on the Co-WIN portal using any of the following ID proofs:Aadhaar card ; Driving License; PAN card; Passport;
Pension Passbook; NPR Smart Card; and Voter ID (EPIC).

4) How can I get a new COVID-19 Vaccination card

If you need a new vaccination card, contact the vaccination provider site where you received your vaccine. Your provider should give you a new card
with up-to-date information about the vaccinations you have received.
If the location where you received your COVID-19 vaccine is no longer operating, contact your state or local health department’s
immunization information system (IIS) for assistance.
CDC does not maintain vaccination records or determine how vaccination records are used, and CDC does not provide the CDC-labeled,
white COVID-19 vaccination record card to people. These cards are distributed to vaccination providers by state and local health departments.
Please contact your state or local health department if you have additional questions about vaccination cards or vaccination records.

5) If I have already had COVID-19 and recovered,do I still need to get vaccinated with a COVID-19 vaccine?

Yes, you should be vaccinated regardless of whether you already had COVID-19 because:
a --> Research has not yet shown how long you are protected from getting COVID-19 again after you recover from COVID-19.
b --> Vaccination helps protect you even if you’ve already had COVID-19

6) Can I choose which COVID-19 vaccine I get?

Yes. All currently authorized and recommended COVID-19 vaccines are safe and effective, and CDC does not recommend one vaccine over another.
The most important decision is to get a COVID-19 vaccination as soon as possible. Widespread vaccination is a critical tool to help stop the pandemic.

7) Can I get vaccinated against COVID-19 while I am currently sick with COVID-19?

No. People with COVID-19 who have symptoms should wait to be vaccinated until they have recovered from their illness and have met the criteria for
discontinuing isolation; those without symptoms should also wait until they meet the criteria before getting vaccinated.
This guidance also applies to people who get COVID-19 before getting their second dose of vaccine.
